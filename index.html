Privacy Policy for Read Later

Last updated: June 4, 2026

Read Later (“the app”) is designed to respect your privacy. This document explains what data the app handles, where it is stored, and how it is used.

Summary

Read Later does not collect personal information about you. The only data it stores is the URLs and page titles you explicitly choose to save. That data lives in your own iCloud account and on your own devices. It is never sent to the developer, never sold, and never shared with any third party.

Data the app stores

When you save a page, the app stores:

  • The URL of the page
  • The title of the page (typically the <title> of the HTML document)
  • A timestamp for when you saved it
  • A timestamp for when you last changed it
  • A read/unread flag

That is the entirety of what is stored. The app does not collect or transmit:

  • Your name, email address, or any account information
  • Device identifiers, advertising identifiers, or IP addresses
  • Your browsing history outside the pages you choose to save
  • Location data
  • Analytics, telemetry, or crash reports of any kind
  • Contacts, calendar, photos, or any other on-device data

Where the data is stored

Saved items are stored in two places, both private to you:

  1. Your iCloud Key-Value Store, so the same list appears on every Apple device signed into the same iCloud account. This is provided by Apple’s NSUbiquitousKeyValueStore API and is governed by Apple’s privacy policy and your iCloud settings.
  2. A local container on each device (an App Group container) so the Safari extension and Share extension can read and write to the list without re-fetching it from iCloud.

The developer has no servers and no access to your data. The only network calls the app makes are to Apple’s iCloud service to sync between your own devices.

Companion apps

A companion Mac app and an optional Chromium browser extension (for Dia, Chrome, Edge, Arc, Brave, etc.) are available separately. Both are designed to operate on the same saved list:

  • The Mac app reads and writes the same iCloud-backed list described above.
  • The Mac app exposes a small HTTP server on localhost:57832 so that the browser extension running on the same Mac can keep its copy of the list in sync. This server is only reachable from the same Mac and accepts no connections from outside the device.
  • The browser extension stores the same fields (URL, title, timestamps, read flag) using the browser’s local extension storage.

Neither companion ever contacts a remote server.

Permissions the iOS app requests

  • iCloud Key-Value Storage — to sync your saved list across your own Apple devices.
  • Share Extension activation — when you choose Read Later from the iOS share sheet, the extension reads the URL being shared so it can save it. It does not read anything else.
  • Safari Web Extension permissions — when enabled in Safari, the extension reads the URL and title of the page you are currently looking at, but only when you tap its toolbar icon.
  • Siri / Shortcuts intent — when you invoke “Save to Read Later” via Siri or Shortcuts, the URL you provide is saved.

Third parties

Read Later does not use any third-party analytics, advertising, crash-reporting, attribution, or tracking SDKs. There are no third parties with access to your data.

Your control over your data

Because your data is stored in your own iCloud and on your own devices, you control it completely:

  • Delete an individual saved item from within the app at any time.
  • Uninstall the app, which removes all locally stored data on that device.
  • Remove the app’s iCloud data via Settings → [your name] → iCloud → Manage Account Storage → Read Later → Delete Data.

Children’s privacy

The app is not directed to children under 13 and does not knowingly collect any personal information from anyone, regardless of age.
